Why Start a Chapter?

By establishing an Apex Rise chapter, you become a catalyst for change in your community, empowering children with confidence and emotional resilience.

1

Local Impact

Directly transform the lives of children in your own community by providing them with essential emotional skills.

2

Community Leadership

Establish yourself as a leader in child development and emotional education in your area.

3

Support Network

Join a nationwide network of chapter leaders with access to training, resources, and mentorship.

How It Works

Our simple 4-step process makes it easy to bring Apex Rise to your community.

1

Application

Complete our chapter application form and tell us about your community and your motivation.

2

Training

Participate in our comprehensive 2-week online training program for chapter leaders.

3

Launch

Receive our curriculum, materials, and ongoing support to launch your chapter.

4

Grow

Start with a small group and expand your impact as you gain experience and volunteers.

Chapter Leader Benefits

  • Comprehensive training in emotional intelligence and child development
  • Access to all Apex Rise program materials and curriculum
  • Monthly mentorship calls with experienced chapter leaders
  • Marketing materials and templates for community outreach
  • Annual leadership conference invitation
  • Certificate of recognition upon successful chapter establishment

Chapter Requirements

To ensure quality and impact, we look for these qualifications in our chapter leaders.

Essential Requirements

  • Passion for child development and emotional empowerment
  • Commitment to run the chapter for at least 1 year
  • Ability to gather at least 10 children for regular sessions
  • Basic English language skills (program materials are in English)
  • Regular internet access for training and support
  • Two references from community leaders or educators

Nice-to-Have Qualities

  • Background in education, psychology, or social work
  • Previous experience working with children
  • Connections with local schools or community centers
  • Ability to recruit and manage volunteers
  • Basic computer skills for reporting and communication
  • Creative ideas for adapting programs to local needs
